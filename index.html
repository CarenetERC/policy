1) Corporate Credit Card Policy (FN08)

Purpose

Set clear expectations for the issuance, use, safeguarding, and monitoring of corporate credit cards.

Scope

All Team Members who are issued a company card or temporarily sign out a card.

Policy Statement

Corporate credit cards are provided for authorized business expenses only. Cardholders and anyone signing out a card are responsible for compliant use, timely documentation, and card security. Carenet pays approved balances in full each month.

Roles & Responsibilities

  • Cardholder / Borrower: Uses the card only for approved business expenses; secures the card; submits receipts and expense reports on time; promptly reports lost/stolen cards; reviews monthly statements.
  • Supervisors: Ensure compliance and timely approvals.
  • Finance Department (Plan Administrator): Administers the program, audits expenses, handles issuance/limits, and may suspend or cancel privileges.

Eligibility & Approval

  • Corporate cards may be issued to full‑time Team Members who frequently travel, purchase minor goods/services, or otherwise incur recurring business expenses.
  • Eligible Team Members request a card through the CFO / Plan Administrator. Cards must be signed immediately upon receipt.

Temporary Card Sign‑Out

Team Members not issued a card may sign out a card from Finance for a specific, approved purchase. Cards must be signed out for each transaction and returned promptly. Storing card information for later use or using a card without signing it out is prohibited and may result in loss of privilege or other discipline.

Permitted Use & Security

  • Business use only. Personal charges are prohibited.
  • Keep spend within approved budgets and limits.
  • Do not transfer a card to others or share card credentials.
  • Do not save card numbers in browsers, vendor accounts, or mobile apps.
  • Use reputable sites with encryption for online transactions.
  • Report lost or stolen cards immediately to American Express and Finance.

Submitting Expense Reports

  • Cardholders: Submit all expenses (corporate card transactions, mileage, and reimbursable out-of-pocket expenses) via Emburse Professional.
  • Non-cardholders: Submit completed & approved Expense Report Form (Excel) and all associated receipts via email to ap@carenethealthcare.com.

Receipts & Expense Reporting

  • Maintain itemized receipts for every transaction. Include: date, vendor, description, total, business purpose, and attendees (with titles/affiliation) when applicable.
  • Cardholders should upload receipts through Emburse as expenses are incurred, and submit expense report by the required monthly deadline.
  • Failure to provide adequate documentation may result in payroll deduction, denial of reimbursement, and/or disciplinary action.

Monthly Review & Charge Verification

Finance downloads charges and uploads them to Emburse twice per month (middle and end of month). Cardholders must review promptly. Reminders will be sent if an expense report is not received within 2–3 business days of notification. Repeated non‑compliance may result in card cancellation and other disciplinary actions.

Disputes

Cardholders are responsible for initiating and tracking disputes with the Card Issuer and notifying Finance/CFO. Ensure proper credits are received on subsequent statements.

Violations (Illustrative)

  • Cash advances or cash‑like transactions.
  • Personal purchases.
  • Allowing unauthorized users to transact.
  • Failing to return a signed‑out card; leaving a card unattended.
  • Not submitting receipts/expense reports by deadlines.
  • Storing card numbers on devices or websites.

Consequences: Disciplinary action up to and including termination; recovery of improper charges; suspension/cancellation of privileges.

Cancellation / Separation

Upon resignation or termination, return the card to Finance immediately and reconcile all outstanding transactions. Departing cardholders are responsible for settling the account prior to departure.

2) Travel, Business Expense & Expense Reports – U.S. (FN02)

Purpose

Ensure business travel and general business expenses are reasonable, necessary, and cost‑effective.

Scope

All U.S.‑based Team Members incurring business travel or general business expenses.

Policy Statement

Use Carenet’s travel software for booking. Charge expenses to a corporate card whenever possible. If a corporate card cannot be used, properly documented, Finance‑approved expenses will be reimbursed.

Pre‑Travel Approval

  • Submit a Travel Request Form at least 10 business days before anticipated travel.
  • Obtain Department VP approval before booking.
  • Provide the approved form to Finance (for notification and any approved advances).

Booking Standards

  • Air: Book the lowest available economy fare; no upgrades, early‑boarding, or optional fees without pre‑approval and justification.
  • Hotel / Car: Book via company travel software unless a demonstrably lower rate is obtained elsewhere (e.g., bona fide loyalty rates). Notify Leadership and Finance of exceptions.
  • Travelers must make changes promptly and within policy.

Documentation & Submission

  • Via Travel Software: Submit an expense report and upload itemized receipts for each transaction (date, total, category, business purpose, attendees when applicable). Airline passenger receipts (not itineraries) are required.
  • Outside of Travel Software: Submit an expense report with itemized receipts and the approved Travel Request Form. Indicate method of payment for each item.
  • Deadlines:
    • Credit card expenses: completed expense report and all receipts due via Emburse within 2-3 days of the credit card statement close date.
    • Out‑of‑pocket expenses: reimbursed on the next payroll if submitted before payroll processing (usually the Friday prior to pay date).
    • Reports >30 days old require Finance approval and may be denied.
  • By submitting/approving an expense report, the Team Member and approving Leader attest to accuracy and completeness and authorize recovery of any overpayment.

Allowable Expenses (Illustrative)

  • Lowest‑fare air or rail; airport transportation.
  • Lodging; internet when necessary for business.
  • Meals (see limits).
  • Limited gratuities within tipping guidance.
  • Laundry/dry cleaning when travel exceeds 5 consecutive nights.
  • Mileage at current IRS rate when a personal vehicle is used for out‑of‑town travel.
  • Rental cars, rideshare services, or taxis when necessary for out‑of‑town travel.
  • Long‑term parking; tolls.

Non‑Reimbursable (Illustrative)

  • Upgrades/optional airline fees
  • In‑room entertainment
  • Laundry for trips of ≤5 nights
  • Books/magazines
  • Alcohol (see Alcohol below)
  • Toiletries
  • Apparel
  • Travel insurance
  • Fines/penalties
  • Vehicle maintenance/repairs
  • Personal property loss
  • Excess baggage (except business materials)
  • Financial/card fees
  • Child care
  • Valet/concierge
  • Minibar
  • Personal phone charges beyond limits
  • Briefcases and similar personal items
  • Health club/sauna
  • Non‑business transportation
  • Personal services
  • Short‑term airport parking for overnight/longer trips.

Meals & Gratuities

  • Reimbursed at actuals, not per diem.
  • Combined daily limit (including tips): $60/day.
  • Partial‑day guidance: Breakfast $10 / Lunch $20 / Dinner $30.
  • Acceptable tip range: up to 15%. Tips above this range may be limited.
  • Use provided meals at events; do not request reimbursement for meals already included in registration fees.

Alcohol

Not reimbursable. Limited, pre‑approved exceptions may apply for bona fide client/vendor business. See Team Member Handbook.

Entertainment

Client/prospect‑related only. Department VPs designate who may incur entertainment expenses and under what circumstances.

Lodging

Reimbursed when an overnight stay is required. Select economical options that support the business purpose.

Ground Transportation

Use the most economical option that meets the business need. Rideshare and shuttle options are preferred for short/limited needs. Taxis only when they are the best choice. If choosing rideshare to the airport instead of parking, the cost should not exceed long‑term parking.

Parking

Use long‑term airport parking. If short‑term parking is used, reimbursement may be limited to the prevailing long‑term daily rate (e.g., SAT up to $16/day as of 07/2023).

Personal Automobile

Reimbursed at the IRS mileage rate for business miles. Mileage to/from the airport is reimbursed only to the extent it exceeds the normal commute. Maintain a contemporaneous mileage log (date, origin/destination, business purpose). Vehicle operating costs are deemed covered by the mileage rate and are not reimbursed separately. Proof of auto liability insurance is required.

Telephone

Hotel long‑distance charges are generally not allowed.

Laundry & Dry Cleaning

Reimbursable when travel exceeds 5 consecutive nights (or if an assignment is unexpectedly extended and documented). Applies only while traveling—not after returning home.

Cash Advances

Available for infrequent travelers upon request on the Travel Request Form. Only one outstanding advance at a time. Any unused/unsupported amount is recovered via payroll deduction.

Work‑From‑Home (WFH) Expenses

No reimbursement is offered where no incremental cost is incurred. In states that require reimbursement of necessary business expenses (California, Iowa, Montana, Massachusetts, New York, Illinois), submit a monthly expense report in Emburse Professional with the service bill showing the service address matching the employee’s address. For mixed personal/business services (e.g., home internet), expense only the business‑use percentage, capped at 50% of the monthly charge. Submit within 30 days of billing.

Staff Appreciation

Obtain VP approval before incurring Staff Appreciation expenses. VPs must manage to budget; unapproved spend may not be reimbursed.

Spouse/Guest Travel

Permitted only with prior approval. Any incremental costs (hotel, transportation, meals, etc.) are the Team Member’s responsibility.

Travel to Corporate Offices

Limit to essential trips when virtual options are not feasible. If corporate office staff dine with traveling Team Members, charge those meals to the Staff Appreciation budget.

3) Gift Support Form Procedure (FN07)

Purpose

Ensure timely, accurate recording and reporting of gifts/gift cards purchased for Team Members, whether via corporate card or reimbursable funds.

Policy Statement

All gifts and gift cards must be documented and submitted to Payroll for reporting. The company will remit appropriate taxes. Finance uses gift records to validate purchases/reimbursements, ensure accurate reporting, and mitigate fraud.

Required Documentation

  • Gift Support Form (Excel) listing recipient name, employee ID, salary/hourly status, and amount; recipient signatures (or Director validation when remote).
  • Receipt for the purchase.
  • Approved expense report (or Emburse Professional transaction reference for corporate card purchases).

Procedure – Expected (Gifts purchased and distributed in the same month)

  1. At purchase: scan and email to Payroll (a) original receipt, (b) approved expense report/Emburse Professional reference, (c) completed Gift Support Form.
  2. Submit the full packet to Finance (corporate card or personal reimbursement, as applicable).

Procedure – Alternate (Gifts distributed over multiple months)

  1. At purchase: attach a copy of the receipt to the Gift Support Form for tracking; submit the original receipt with the expense report/Emburse Professional reference to Finance.
  2. As gifts are distributed: record each distribution and obtain signatures.
  3. Monthly until all gifts from that receipt are distributed: send the updated Gift Support Form to Payroll (with the receipt copy) showing all distributions made that month.

4) Gym/Fitness Program Reimbursement Policy (FN04)

Purpose

Encourage wellness by standardizing reimbursement for eligible fitness programs.

Policy Statement

Carenet reimburses up to $27.00 per month for an eligible gym/fitness program when the Team Member attends at least 10 days in the month. Eligible programs must provide verifiable utilization reports (date/time‑stamped), not simple notes.

Eligibility

  • Available after 30 days of employment.
  • Recreational activities without attendance reporting do not qualify.

Process

  • Gold’s Gym (payroll‑deducted): No reimbursement request needed; Gold’s provides utilization reports directly.
  • All other programs: Submit a Gym Expense Form via Jira (Payroll) within 30 days after month‑end with (a) receipt showing monthly fee and (b) utilization/progress report. Calculations are generally processed within 7 business days; reimbursement is paid in the next payroll following processing.

Tax Treatment

Reimbursed amounts are taxable income (FICA/Medicare/federal/state/local as applicable).

5) Philippines (PHL) Travel, Business Expense & Expense Reports (FN10)

Purpose

Provide PHL‑specific travel and expense guidance aligned with Carenet standards and local requirements.

Scope

All Team Members based in the Philippines or traveling from the Philippines on Carenet business.

Policy Statement

Use company travel software for booking. Charge expenses to a corporate card whenever possible. Properly documented, Finance‑approved expenses are reimbursed per this policy.

Approvals & Lead Times

  • Local PHL travel: Submit Travel Request Form and Cash Advance Form 15–30 days before travel.
  • U.S. travel/visas: Submit 2–3 months in advance.

Booking Standards

  • Air: Lowest available economy; no upgrades/early‑boarding/optional fees without pre‑approval and justification.
  • Hotel/Car: Book via company travel software unless demonstrably lower rates are available elsewhere (notify Leadership and Finance).
  • Changes must be made promptly and within policy.

Documentation, Submission & Timing

  • Submit expense reports no later than 5 business days after travel ends.
  • Via Travel Software: Upload itemized receipts for each transaction (date, total, category, purpose, attendees). Passenger receipts required.
  • Outside of Travel Software: Submit itemized receipts, approved Travel Request Form, and method of payment for each item.
  • Reimbursement timing: Same as U.S. policy (after statement close for card charges; next payroll if submitted before payroll processing).
  • Cash advances: If receipts total less than the advance, return the difference via wire transfer. Submission/approval attests to accuracy and authorizes recovery of overpayments.

Allowable Expenses (Illustrative)

Lowest‑fare air/rail; airport transport; lodging; internet when necessary; meals (see limits); limited gratuities; laundry/dry cleaning for trips >5 nights; rideshare as necessary; rental cars only with one‑off approval.

Non‑Reimbursable (Illustrative)

Same as U.S. list, including upgrades/optional airline fees; entertainment; apparel; fines/penalties; financial/card fees; personal/concierge services; minibar; short‑term airport parking, etc.

Meals & Gratuities (PHL)

  • Reimbursed at actuals. Not per diem.
  • Local PHL travel: daily combined limit ₱350 (including tips).
  • U.S. travel: daily combined limit $65 USD (including tips).
  • Partial‑day guidance: Breakfast $10 / Lunch $20 / Dinner $35 (USD).
  • Acceptable tips: up to 15%; excess may be limited.
  • Use provided meals at events; do not request reimbursement for meals already included.

Lodging

Reimbursable when an overnight stay is required. For Company events in the U.S., use the leadership‑designated hotel block.

Ground Transportation

Rideshare is preferred for short/limited needs (Uber Comfort or Uber XL for groups up to 6). Rental cars require one‑off approval. Personal/leisure transportation is not reimbursable.

Air Travel – Philippine Travel Tax (TIEZA)

The Philippine Travel Tax applies to outbound international trips under PD 1183.
Payment options:

  • If the ticket is issued outside the Philippines: pay at the TIEZA Travel Tax counter at the airport.
  • If issued in the Philippines: pay via airline ticket office/travel agency.
  • If purchased online: either pay during booking (adult passengers only) or at the airport TIEZA counter before check‑in.

Requirement: Submit the travel‑tax receipt with your expense report.

Telephone

Use WhatsApp where possible. Hotel long‑distance charges are not allowed.

Laundry & Dry Cleaning

Reimbursable when travel exceeds 5 consecutive nights (see U.S. policy for details).

Staff Appreciation

Requires VP approval in advance. Unapproved spend may not be reimbursed.

Spouse/Guest Travel

Permitted only with prior approval. Any incremental costs are the Team Member’s responsibility.

Travel to Corporate Offices

Limit to essential trips when virtual options are not feasible. Corporate‑office meals with traveling Team Members are charged to Staff Appreciation.
